Cosmos

Cosmos is a web game -- created entirely using Javascript, HTML, and CSS -- that allows the user to interactively traverse a 'galaxy' on any browser-supported device. The user is a ball in the galaxy with the goal of surviving the longest (HIGH SCORE). You survive by avoiding contact with any bouncing white invaders.

Playing in SNIPER mode increases difficulty but comes with the ability to shoot and defeat the bubbles.

It was an enlightening experience creating the app - a relatively ambitious project for my first experience with the web and JavaScript.

Leonhard Euler (/ˈɔɪlər/ oy-lər;[2] Swiss Standard German [ˈɔɪlər] ( listen), German Standard German [ˈɔʏlɐ] ( listen)) (15 April 1707 – 18 September 1783) was a Swiss mathematician, physicist, astronomer, logician and engineer who made important and influential discoveries in many branches of mathematics like infinitesimal calculus and graph theory while also making pioneering contributions to several branches such as topology and analytic number theory. He also introduced much of the modern mathematical terminology and notation, particularly for mathematical analysis, such as the notion of a mathematical function.[3] He is also known for his work in mechanics, fluid dynamics, optics, astronomy, and music theory.[4]

Euler was one of the most eminent mathematicians of the 18th century, and is held to be one of the greatest in history. He is also widely considered to be the most prolific mathematician of all time. His collected works fill 60 to 80 quarto volumes,[5] more than anybody in the field. He spent most of his adult life in St. Petersburg, Russia, and in Berlin, then the capital of Prussia.
